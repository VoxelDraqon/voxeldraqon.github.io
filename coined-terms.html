Terms I've Coined

These are some terms I have coined myself. Most are also viewable on the LGBTA Wiki.

Aslangender

Aslan is a character from the Chronicles of Narina by C.S. Lewis. He is a depiction of Jesus in the form of a sapient, talking lion. Aslangender is a gender that feels fully xenic or outherine, but also fully male, female, or neutral. This is similar to how Jesus is said to have been fully man and fully god.
Aslangender can also refer to a gender that feels like the fabric of the universe or has connections to lions, Narnia, the fantasy genre, and/or religion/spirituality.
coined 17 October, 2020; flag by me

Dracogender

Dracogender is a xenogender tied to dragons. This can manifest in multiple possible ways: an otherkin person's kintype is a dragon and this is their kingender; a person's gender feels large, scaly, winged, and possibly related to fire or water; a person's gender feels like a literal dragon.
This gender can also be combined with other gendered terms to make things such as dracoboy or femdracogender.
coined 7 November, 2020; flag by me

Hardwaregender

Hardwaregender is a xenogender defined by a connection to computer hardware, such as circuit boards, wires, and CPUs. It may also involve a connection to a specific type of computer, in which case it can overlap with OSgender.
flag by anonymous FANDOM user

Sméagender

Sméagender is a xenogender linked to the character Sméagol/Gollum from J.R.R. Tolkien's Middle-Earth. It feels almost like a person, but not quite, or like it was a person but isn't anymore. It feels small and animalistic and controlling, and reminds one of dark, damp things, and long, thin fingers. It feels like it had been around for a very long time and knows too much, but is weak at the same time. It is like the devil on your shoulder.
A Sméagender individual may feel more comfortable referring to themselves as we/us, despite being one person.
coined 6 November, 2020 (my birthday lol); flag by me

Sociagender

Sociagender, YouTubegender, or Tubegender is a xenogender and technogender connected to or influenced by YouTube, Twitch, other video sharing/streaming platforms, and/or the communities/cultures surrounding them. It can also be used to refer to a xenogender that relates to the content/persona of a specific content creator or the community surrounding them.
coined 1 February, 2021; flag by Jade the weird random bigender lesbian
